Event 5: LinkedIn: Scrum Basics

Date: 15/06/2025

Summary

Another LinkedIn Learning activity that I completed online. This one was an introductory lesson on Scrum, however it was from the perspective of someone already in the industry, and why/how others in the industry are able to adopt this newer agile framework. I found this perspective to be quite important - we get taught why agile is better at Uni, but it is different hearing from someone who faced real world challenges that agile helped overcome. The motivating factor was the ability to have a regular deliverable, and have the ability to ‘fail fast’. With an agile framework, companies can know early on if a project is worth the time and money, rather than having to wait for development to finish.

Additional Comments

It was also highlighted how brief and direct scrum meetings are meant to be, to the point of no one even sitting down. This was covered in another module we did this semester, but the idea of trust being essential for teams was mentioned, as it is important for the team to know if you have failed to meet some expectation, either set by yourself, sprint master or stakeholder, and you need to feel comfortable enough to share what has happened, rather than try to hide it.
